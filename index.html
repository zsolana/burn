Shielded Coin

Toly's meme of zcash since 2013

The Origin

In the wild world of Solana Twitter, where Toly and Mert exchange their legendary banter, a new meme was born that would become an instant classic.

The "Shielded Coin" joke emerged from Toly's playful trolling of Mert Mumtaz, the CEO of Helius and vocal Zcash supporter, poking fun at the irony of Zcash's privacy features.

While Zcash claims to offer "shielded" transactions for ultimate privacy, Toly turned this on its head—suggesting the coin itself has no protection against exploits, making it the perfect setup for crypto's favorite inside joke.

What is the Shielded Coin Meme?

The "Shielded Coin" meme is Toly's brilliant wordplay that captures the absurdity of cryptocurrency marketing versus reality.

Zcash uses "shielded pools" for privacy—but Toly jokes that while your transactions might be shielded, the protocol itself apparently isn't shielded from much of anything.

It's become Toly's go-to way to troll Mert whenever Zcash comes up in conversation, creating one of the most entertaining ongoing bits in crypto Twitter history. The Zcash community has embraced it too, turning the joke into a badge of honor.

Why This Matters

We're witnessing peak crypto culture where the Solana co-founder and one of the ecosystem's biggest builders can publicly roast each other's favorite projects while maintaining genuine respect.

The "Shielded Coin" meme proves that in 2025, the best crypto communities don't take themselves too seriously—even when discussing privacy tech and security models.

This isn't just about Zcash—it's about how the Solana community has mastered the art of high-level technical debate mixed with absolutely unhinged meme warfare. Toly's legendary trolling game keeps everyone on their toes.
